I’d give this paper 83/100.

Strengths
- The paper tackles a real and important systems problem: Kubernetes’ fixed termination grace period does not match the variable time required for stateful BEAM services to drain, hand off state, and converge.
- The core technical idea is strong and practical, because it uses runtime signals to set grace dynamically instead of guessing a constant in advance.
- The evaluation supports the main claim: fixed grace periods can lose state under heavier load, while the adaptive controller avoids that loss and reduces over-provisioning.

Technical quality
- The safety condition is simple and intuitive: graceful termination is safe only when the grace period covers drain, handoff, and convergence time.
- The controller design is logically coherent, and the implementation shows that the idea is not merely theoretical.
- The paper also covers useful dimensions beyond correctness, including efficiency, adaptivity, robustness, and scalability.

Weaknesses
- The main limitation is external validity: most experiments are on a small cluster, which makes production-scale behavior harder to judge.
- The paper is tightly tied to the BEAM/Kubernetes/Horde/libcluster stack, so generality to other runtimes is suggested more than demonstrated.
- Some sections are repetitive, and the presentation is denser than it needs to be.

Writing and presentation
- The paper is well structured overall, but it reads more like a strong technical report than a polished, concise journal article.
- The arguments are persuasive, though they repeat the same central point several times.
- The figures and tables appear to support the claims well, but the paper would benefit from a tighter presentation.

Score rationale
- I would score it high on novelty, engineering relevance, and completeness.
- I would deduct points for the limited experimental scale and the narrow runtime focus.
- Overall, this is a strong systems paper with a useful practical contribution, but not yet a broadly general solution.

- Novelty: 17/20.

- Generality: 13/20.

Final score: 83/100.
